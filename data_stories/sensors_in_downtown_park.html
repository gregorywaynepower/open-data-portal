Sensors in Cary's Downtown Park

Last Updated: {{ downtownparkmap.dataset.metas.metadata_processed | date }}

Downtown Cary Park is the test bed for Internet-of-Things technology. Cary is using a suite of sensors to provide better service to our visitors. The integration of sensor technology in Cary's downtown park exemplifies a commitment to sustainability and innovation. The utilization of sensors transforms Cary's downtown park into a smart and dynamic space, ensuring a seamless blend of technology and nature for the enjoyment of residents and visitors alike.

The data for this report is from Downtown Cary Park's opening weekend spanning November 17 2023 to November 19 2023. All maps and charts are interactive. For charts, hover over points of interest, and click-and-drag inside chart areas to select a particular range you want to get a closer look into.

Sound Sensor Measurements

This dataset contains information reported from a noise sensor located at the Town of Cary Library parking deck. This dataset is used to monitor the noise levels in accordance with Cary's noise ordinance which states sound amplification is to be under 60 decibels between 9 a.m. and 9 p.m. and under 50 decibels between 9 p.m. and 9 a.m.
