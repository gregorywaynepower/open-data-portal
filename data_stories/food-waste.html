In 2019, Cary partnered with Wake County to conduct a Waste Characterization Study, which estimated that 27.4% of Cary's single-family residential waste was food waste. In light of this study's results, Cary began work on a pilot Food Waste Recycling Drop-Off program to help the community divert food waste from the landfill and create compost. The pilot launched on February 7, 2022, Citizen's Convenience Center, 313 N Dixon Ave., for Cary citizens to drop off their food scraps and other compostable items. Accepted items include food scraps (raw or cooked), coffee grounds, paper towels, pizza boxes, and pet food, among other compostable items. Check out the full list of accepted materials on our website.

The pilot became a permanent service in February 2023.

Be sure to check back each month to stay up-to-date on this service's statistics. Check out the full datasets: Food Waste Recycling Impact and Food Waste Recycling Participation.


Impact to Date

What happens after our citizen's food scraps are dropped off? All materials are processed by a local facility, after which some of the finished compost is returned to the earth at Good Hope Farm. So far, the results have been impressive.

Gaining Popularity

We saw a nearly 83% increase in participation between the first and second weeks of the pilot's launch. In the first twelve weeks, we recorded an estimated 2,301 drop-offs, with weekends being our busiest time.
