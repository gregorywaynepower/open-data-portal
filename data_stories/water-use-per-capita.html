Gallons per capita per day (GPCD) is a measure of the average daily water usage per person in a particular area or community. It is typically used to estimate the water demand and consumption of a population and is expressed in gallons per day per person. GPCD can be used by water utilities, policymakers, and researchers to understand water usage patterns, forecast future demand, and identify areas where water conservation efforts may be needed.
