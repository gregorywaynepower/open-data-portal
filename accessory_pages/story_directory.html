CASSI in Cary's Bond Park - Driverless Shuttle Pilot

During a 13-week period from March 6 through June 2, 2023, Fred G. Bond Metro Park was the site of an exciting demonstration in driverless vehicle technology. The North Carolina Department of Transportation (NCDOT) partnered with Cary to bring an all-electric, automated shuttle to Bond Park. The Connected Autonomous Shuttle Supporting Innovation (CASSI) ran a 1.6-mile, four-stop route from the Cary Senior Center to Bond Park Community Center on weekdays from 10:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. during the pilot.

Cary's Water use per Capita

Cary’s conservation strategy began in 1996, a time of extraordinary growth for the town, with the ambitious goal of reducing per person water usage by 20% by 2015. The vision was to support the high quality of life in Cary by continuing to provide safe and reliable water service, but also to steward our natural resources and infrastructure investments. That goal was met earlier than expected, with the 20% reduction mark hit by 2013. Remarkably, this conservation trend has continued with a reduction of 30% by 2023. Additionally, thanks to a steady reduction in maximum day demand, Cary was able to realize significant cost savings. Thanks to impressive community support, this successful journey included multiple droughts, cutting-edge technology, and significant shifts in both industry and development standards during its span.

Solar, Cary, and You

Solar energy is an important part of reducing carbon emissions and building a sustainable and resilient community. According to Cary's Land Development Ordinance, home solar installations, including photovoltaic cells and solar water heaters, are allowed everywhere in Cary. As part of Cary's commitment to excellence in sustainability, the installation of these solar systems is regulated by Cary's building permit process and reviewed for compliance with the LDO and North Carolina Building and Electrical Code. Stormwater Monitoring Dashboard

In February 2020 Cary kicked off its Smart and Connected Community Stormwater Workshop. This 2-day workshop set out to create a scalable and repeatable architecture for near real-time alerting and visualization of rising storm water levels allowing for automated operational response and citizen notification, data sharing with regional partners as well as analysis and prediction of future events. In the almost 3 years since this first workshop Cary has put in place over 30 stream gages and 11 rain gages in the Swift Creek and Walnut Creek basins. Other town owned rain gages have also been incorporated to help build out this network.

Cary Composts

In 2019, Cary partnered with Wake County to conduct a Waste Characterization Study, which estimated that 27.4% of Cary's single-family residential waste was food waste. In light of this study's results, Cary began work on a pilot Food Waste Recycling Drop-Off program to help the community divert food waste from the landfill and create compost.
